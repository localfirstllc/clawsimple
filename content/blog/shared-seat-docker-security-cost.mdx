> "How can the Shared Plan be cheaper? Does cheaper mean less secure?"

If you're wondering how ClawSimple's Shared Plan works behind the scenes, this article will answer three simple questions:

1. How do we keep your AI agents secure when sharing a server?
2. Why is it significantly cheaper than a dedicated server?
3. What are the trade-offs, and how do we protect you from noisy neighbors?

---

## The Core Concept: Your Own Private Room

Imagine moving into an apartment building instead of buying a whole house. You share the overall foundation (the server), but your apartment (your container) has its own locks, its own space, and its own rules.

According to the official OpenClaw architecture, we don't just dump all users into one big chat group. Every single ClawSimple Shared Plan user gets:

- **Their own private container.** 
- **Their own secure gateway.** 
- **Their own independent storage.**

This means your AI agents, your files, and your chat history are completely isolated from everyone else on the same physical server.

---

## Why Is It Cheaper?

When you rent a dedicated server, you're paying for 100% of that computer's power, 24 hours a day. However, most personal AI agents are mostly idle. They only work when you talk to them. It's like paying to keep a taxi running in your driveway just in case you need to go somewhere.

The Shared Plan solves this by splitting the cost:

- We put several secure "apartments" (containers) on one large, powerful server.
- You only pay a fraction of the total server cost.
- We strictly limit the maximum number of users per server, ensuring it never gets overcrowded.

The result? You get the same OpenClaw experience, but your wallet stays much happier. It's the perfect choice for personal use, testing, and everyday automation.

---

## Is It Just As Secure?

Yes, the fundamental security barriers remain identical to a dedicated server.

In ClawSimple’s Shared Plan:
- Strangers cannot access your AI agents. 
- Your workspace is locked down. Other users on the server cannot see your data.
- Nobody (not even you) has raw "root" access to the underlying machine, which further prevents anyone from breaking out of their container to snoop.

While technically speaking, sharing a server foundation is different from owning a standalone physical machine, for 99% of personal and typical business automation tasks, this containerized isolation is an enterprise-standard, highly secure approach.

---

## The Trade-off: The "Noisy Neighbor"

The real trade-off of a shared system isn't security—it's performance.

Because you are sharing the engine of the server, if someone else's AI suddenly starts doing a massive, complex task that eats up a lot of CPU power, your AI might reply a second or two slower. This is a common phenomenon in cloud computing known as the "noisy neighbor" effect.

---

## How We Stop Noisy Neighbors

To make sure one heavy user doesn't ruin the experience for everyone else, we put strict speed limits in place right from the start.

When your Shared Plan is created, we instantly apply hard limits on:
- **CPU (Brain Power):** No single user can hog all the processing power.
- **Memory (RAM):** No single user can eat up all the system memory.
- **Processes:** Limits on how many tasks one user can run simultaneously.

These aren't just marketing promises—they are actual code rules (`--cpus`, `--memory`) burned into your container when it's built.

---

## Conclusion

The ClawSimple Shared Plan is not a "downgraded" product. It's a smarter, more cost-effective way to host your agents.

- Your data remains locked down and private.
- We use strict borders to keep things running smoothly.
- You stop paying for 24/7 idle time.

If you want a low-risk, highly affordable way to start building your AI automations, the Shared Plan is the best way to get moving. You can always click a button to upgrade to a massive dedicated server later when your project takes off!
